Development of word recognition in preschoolers

Development of word recognition in preschoolers

Tristan Mahr

2018-07-17

Abstract

[draft] Vocabulary size in preschool is a robust predictor of later language development, and early language skills predict early literacy skills at school entry. By studying the mechanisms that shape word learning, we can understand how individual differences in language ability arise and identify strategies for closing language gaps between children. Word recognition—the process of mapping incoming speech sounds to known or novel words—has been shown in toddlers to predict later language outcomes. We do not know how this ability develops over time, and we do not know when word recognition is most predictive of future outcomes. This project will provide an integrated account of how word recognition develops from age 3 to age 5.
